Book Cover Redesign

Book Cover Redesign with flaps

DETAILS

The objectives of this project were to re-create an already designed book cover of your choice that would effectively communicate the book's essence and inspire the reader to stop, look, and buy the book. The client asked that the themes of the book were portrayed in the cover and that there are 2 good choices available. I chose the book Letters to a Young Poet because it's one of my all time favorite books. I always start my projects with extensive research. I researched already existing book covers for this particular book as well as inspirational book covers to draw ideas from. I then gathered overall themes of the book. Some of the themese include: having patience with yourself, the importance of solitude and introspection, living life's questions, and connecting to your higher self to inhance your creativity. I included several symbolisms to portray the many themese of the book. The purple and orange star flowers are alium and aster flower respectively. They both symbolize widsom which is an overaching theme throughout. The eyes represent the third eye which is symbolism for mystical intuition and insight. The mushrooms symbolize enlightenment, rebirth, and transformations. The yin and yang represent inner balance. The question marks inside the alium flower represents the author's advice to embrace the questions of life. The coral symbolizes patience because it takes a long time to grow. I used all of these symbols and weaved then into a beautiful pattern that spans the front, back, and spine. I chose bright colors that would draw the reader in and finally I used a clean sans serif typeface to tie everything together. I was able to provide two successful designs which the client loved but ultimately chose the pink/purple one. This project was a success in portraying all of the themes in an aesthetically pleasing way. It also successfully draws people in and intice them to pick up the book. This was an overall fun project to design for!
